Common Car Lockout Situations We Handle

Car lockouts happen in many ways. Here are the most common scenarios our Queens customers call us about:

  • Keys locked in the car — The classic lockout. You step out and the door locks behind you with the keys on the seat or in the ignition.
  • Keys locked in the trunk — You place your keys in the trunk while loading items and close it without thinking.
  • Broken key in the lock — Your key breaks off inside the door lock or ignition, leaving you unable to enter or start the car.
  • Key fob battery dead — Your electronic key fob stops working and you cannot unlock the doors or start the push-button ignition.
  • Child or pet locked inside — An urgent situation where a child or pet is locked inside the vehicle and immediate entry is critical.
  • Lost keys — You have lost your keys entirely and need to gain entry to retrieve personal items or have the vehicle towed to a dealer.

Our Lockout Tools and Techniques

Our technicians carry a complete set of professional vehicle entry tools that are designed to unlock cars without causing any damage. These include air wedges that gently create a small gap between the door and frame, long-reach tools that can press interior unlock buttons or pull door handles, slim jim tools for older vehicles with manual locks, and specialized decoder picks for specific lock types. For newer vehicles with advanced electronic security systems, we use manufacturer-specific entry methods that bypass the lock without triggering alarms or causing system errors.

Every tool and technique we use is non-destructive. We take pride in leaving your vehicle exactly as it was before the lockout, with no scratches, dents, or damage of any kind. Our success rate is extremely high — we can get into virtually any vehicle on the road.
